## Introduction

Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) involves gathering and analyzing publicly available data to produce actionable intelligence. This repository aims to provide a structured approach to OSINT, with tutorials and tools that cover various aspects of the field.

## Getting Started

### What is OSINT?

OSINT stands for Open-Source Intelligence. It is the practice of collecting and analyzing information from publicly available sources to gain insights or intelligence about a target or topic.

### Why OSINT?

OSINT is crucial for cybersecurity, investigations, journalism, and more. It allows for the discovery of information that may not be easily accessible but is available online.

### Ethical Considerations

OSINT must be conducted ethically and legally. Always respect privacy laws and the terms of service of the platforms you use.

## Special Content

### Social Media OSINT

- **Where:** Identifying the location of posts, photos, or videos through social media check-ins, tagged locations, and contextual clues.
- **When:** Determining the exact or approximate time a post was made through timestamps, sun position in images, and event timing.
- **Who:** Identifying individuals through username tracking, facial recognition, and network mapping.

### Photo OSINT

- **Where:** Using reverse image search and EXIF data to locate where a photo was taken.
- **When:** Analyzing shadows, weather patterns, and metadata to determine when a photo was taken.
- **Who:** Utilizing facial recognition, clothing, and social context to identify people in photos.

### Video OSINT

- **Where:** Identifying the location in videos using landmarks, map overlays, and visual clues.
- **When:** Extracting timestamps, analyzing environmental cues, and using frame analysis to determine when a video was recorded.
- **Who:** Identifying people through facial recognition, voice analysis, and contextual observation.
